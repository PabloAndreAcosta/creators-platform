# Market Pricing Analysis for Usha Platform
## Swedish Creative Marketplace — Competitor Pricing Models (2025-2026)

---

## 1. EVENTBRITE — Event Marketplace

**Model:** Per-ticket fees (no subscription required for basic use)

| Fee Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| Service fee | 3.7% + $1.79 per ticket |
| Payment processing | 2.9% per order |
| In-person sales | $1 per ticket + 2.99% |
| Free events | $0 |
| Pro plan (optional marketing tools) | From $15/month |
| **Effective total fee per ticket** | **~10-14%** |

**Who pays:** Fees are shown to buyers at checkout by default, but organizers can choose to absorb them.

**Key details:**
- No subscription required — platform is free for publishing events
- Fees only apply to paid tickets
- Previously had a "Flex" per-event plan; now discontinued
- Pro plan ($15-$100/month) adds email marketing tools and human support
- Fee cap was removed (previously $19.95); no ceiling on per-ticket fees
- Eventbrite no longer refunds their fees on canceled/refunded tickets

**Accessibility vs Monetization:** Very low barrier to entry (free to list). Monetizes purely on transaction success. This means organizers take zero risk until they sell.

## 2. FIVERR — Creative Services Marketplace

**Model:** Flat commission on both sides

| Fee Component | Who Pays | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Seller commission | Seller/Creator | **20% flat** on all earnings (including tips) |
| Buyer service fee | Buyer | **5.5%** of purchase price |
| Small order fee | Buyer | Additional **$2** on orders under $50 |

**Tiered system:** None for standard sellers. The 20% is uniform regardless of volume or tenure.

**Exception:** Logo designers face a newer tier system (March 2025+) with commissions ranging from **20-50%** based on Logo Maker tool usage.

**Seller levels (affect visibility, not commission):**
- New Seller → Level 1 → Level 2 → Top Rated Seller
- Top Rated and Seller Plus Premium: 7-day payout (vs. 14 days standard)

**Key takeaway for Usha:** Fiverr's 20% is widely considered steep. It works because of massive buyer traffic (80M+ monthly visitors). Smaller platforms cannot justify 20%.

## 3. UPWORK — Freelance Services Marketplace

**Model:** Variable commission + optional seller subscription + buyer fees

### Freelancer (Seller) Fees
| Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| Service fee (new system, May 2025+) | **0-15% variable** (based on skill category, supply/demand) |
| Effective average for most freelancers | **~10%** |
| Previous tiered system (legacy) | 20% on first $500/client → 10% on $500-$10K → 5% above $10K |

### Freelancer Plus Subscription
| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Cost | **$19.99/month** ($27 via Apple App Store) |
| Includes | 100 Connects/month, AI assistant, competitor bid visibility |
| Direct contracts fee | **0%** (vs. 5% for free-tier users) |

### Client (Buyer) Fees
| Plan | Fee |
|---|---|
| Marketplace plan | **3-5%** on all payments to freelancers |
| Business Plus plan | **8-10%** on all payments |
| Contract initiation fee | One-time fee per new contract |

**Key takeaway for Usha:** Upwork charges BOTH sides. The buyer fee (3-10%) is notable — most marketplaces only charge one side visibly.

## 4. AIRBNB EXPERIENCES — Experience Marketplace

**Model:** Host-only fee (changed in late 2025)

| Fee Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| **Experiences** host service fee | **20%** of the experience price |
| **Stays** host-only fee (new model) | **15.5%** (standardized Oct 2025) |
| Guest service fee (old model) | Eliminated — now absorbed into host fee |

**Key change (Oct-Dec 2025):** Airbnb moved from a split-fee model (3% host + 14-16% guest) to a unified **15.5% host-only fee**. Guests now see the final price with no separate service fee at checkout.

**For Experiences specifically:** The 20% commission remains. This is one of the highest in the experiences category.

**Key takeaway for Usha:** The trend is toward transparent, all-inclusive pricing where the seller absorbs the fee and the buyer sees a clean price. The 20% on experiences is considered high but justified by Airbnb's global distribution.

## 5. CLASSPASS — Fitness/Wellness Booking

**Model:** Buyer membership (credit-based) + studio revenue share

### Consumer Membership Tiers (US, 2026)
| Tier | Monthly Cost | Credits |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | $19/month | 8 credits |
| Entry | $29-$49/month | ~14-28 credits |
| Default (auto-renew after trial) | $59/month | 28 credits |
| Classic | $99-$149/month | ~50-75 credits |
| Elite | $149-$249/month | ~75-125 credits |

*Credits required per class vary by studio popularity, time, and location.*

### Studio/Partner Side
| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Subscription fee to list | **$0** (free to join) |
| Revenue model | Revenue share per visit; studios set a confidential "rate floor" |
| Typical per-visit payout (US) | **$10-$30** per ClassPass visit (vs. $25-$40 drop-in rate) |
| Revenue split | Commonly **50/50 to 70/30** (studio/ClassPass) |
| SmartRate optimization | Dynamic pricing that fills empty slots; ~20% higher payouts for users |

**Key takeaway for Usha:** ClassPass is the strongest example of a **buyer-pays membership** model. Studios accept lower per-visit revenue in exchange for filling empty capacity. This model works for services with perishable inventory (empty class slots).

## 6. BOOKSY — Service Booking Platform

**Model:** Monthly subscription + new-client commission (optional)

| Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| Base subscription | **$29.99/month** (first user) |
| Additional staff members | **$10-$20/month** each |
| Unlimited staff plan | **$119.99/month** |
| Boost (optional marketing) commission | **30%** of first visit from new clients acquired through Boost |
| Standard marketplace bookings | **No commission** |
| Payment processing | **2.49-2.69%** + transaction fees |

**Key details:**
- 14-day free trial
- No commission on organic marketplace bookings — only on Boost-acquired new clients
- Subscription model means predictable costs for providers

**Key takeaway for Usha:** Booksy separates "platform access" (subscription) from "marketing/acquisition" (commission on new clients only). This is a clean model that providers find fair.

## 7. FRESHA — Beauty/Wellness Booking

**Model:** Monthly subscription + first-visit marketplace commission

| Component | Rate |
|---|---|
| Individual plan | **$19.95/month** |
| Team plan | **$14.95/month per team member** |
| Marketplace new-client commission | **20%** (one-time, on first appointment only; minimum $6) |
| Repeat client bookings | **$0** |
| Payment processing | **2.9% + $0.30** per transaction |

**Key details:**
- Previously was fully free (subscription-free until 2025 change)
- The 20% commission is ONLY for brand-new clients discovered via Fresha marketplace
- All direct bookings and repeat clients: zero commission

**Key takeaway for Usha:** The "first visit only" commission model is powerful — it aligns incentives (platform earns only when it delivers genuine new business) and feels fair to providers.

## 8. FEVER — Experiences/Events Platform

**Model:** Negotiated commission per partnership (not publicly fixed)

| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Listing events | Free (via 0% Commission Link) |
| Commission on ticket sales | **Negotiated per partnership** (estimated 15-30%) |
| Fee positioning | Described as "marketing fee" rather than ticketing fee |
| Additional revenue | In-app advertising, premium/promoted listings |
| Buyer fees | Included in ticket price; additional checkout fee reported |

**Key details:**
- Fever acquired DICE in June 2025
- Commission reflects marketing + distribution + tech + customer support
- Commissions reportedly "3-5x a standard ticketing fee" = estimated **15-25%**
- Strong AI/data-driven approach to marketing events

**Key takeaway for Usha:** Fever's model shows that if you deliver strong marketing/distribution value, you can justify higher commissions. The "marketing fee" framing (vs. "ticketing fee") is a smart positioning choice.

## 9. DICE — Event/Ticket Platform

**Model:** All-in pricing (fees bundled into ticket price)

| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Published fee rate | **Custom/negotiated** per promoter/venue |
| Historical booking fee | ~**10%** on tickets above £10 (2018 reference) |
| Fee structure | Split between DICE and the event promoter/venue |
| Annual fee cap increase | Up to **5%** increase per year allowed in contracts |

**Key details:**
- Originally launched as "no booking fees" — absorbed all costs (and lost money on every ticket)
- Shifted to including booking fees as they scaled
- "The price you see is the price you pay" — fees are bundled, not itemized
- Acquired by Fever in June 2025
- Anti-scalping features; no ticket transfers on secondary markets

**Key takeaway for Usha:** DICE's "clean price" philosophy (no surprise fees at checkout) is strongly consumer-friendly and reduces cart abandonment. Consider this approach.

## 10. PATREON — Creator Platform

**Model:** Percentage-based platform fee (tiered by plan)

### Current Fee Structure (Post August 4, 2025)
| Plan | Platform Fee | Who |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (new creators) | **10%** of earnings | All new creators |
| Legacy Pro (existing creators) | **8%** | Grandfathered |
| Legacy Premium (existing) | **~11%** (reduced from 12%) | Grandfathered |
| Merch add-on | **+3%** additional | Optional |

### Additional Fees
| Fee | Rate |
|---|---|
| Payment processing (US) | **2.9% + $0.30** per pledge |
| Currency conversion | **2.5%** |
| Apple iOS in-app | **30%** (Apple's cut on iOS purchases) |

**Key details:**
- No buyer/patron fees — creators absorb all platform costs
- Previously had Lite (5%), Pro (8%), Premium (12%) tiers
- Consolidated to single 10% tier for simplicity
- All-in cost for a creator: roughly **13-16%** (platform + processing + currency)

**Key takeaway for Usha:** Patreon shows the trend toward simplified, single-tier pricing. Their move from 3 tiers to 1 tier suggests that complexity in pricing plans creates friction.

## STRATEGIC INSIGHTS FOR USHA

### 1. Commission Model Options

**Option A: Transaction-only (Eventbrite/Fiverr model)**
- 0 subscription, 10-15% per transaction
- Pros: Zero barrier for creators, easy to understand
- Cons: No predictable revenue, creators feel the cut on every sale

**Option B: Subscription + low commission (Booksy/Bokadirekt model)**
- 199-499 SEK/month + 3-5% per transaction
- Pros: Predictable revenue, creators pay less per transaction
- Cons: Higher barrier to entry, need to demonstrate value before first sale

**Option C: Freemium + new-client commission (Fresha/Timma model)**
- Free basic listing + 15-20% commission only on new clients from marketplace
- Pros: Aligns platform incentive with creator success, fair perception
- Cons: Revenue only comes from growth, not ongoing usage

**Option D: Buyer membership + lower seller fees (ClassPass model)**
- Audience pays 99-249 SEK/month for credits/access
- Creators pay lower commission (5-10%)
- Pros: Dual revenue stream, buyer lock-in, lower creator resistance
- Cons: Requires large supply to justify buyer membership

### 2. Who Should Pay?

| Approach | Used By | Usha Consideration |
|---|---|---|
| Seller pays all | Airbnb (new model), Patreon | Clean buyer experience; sellers price accordingly |
| Buyer pays all | Billetto, Eventbrite (default) | Transparent for sellers; buyers see added costs |
| Both pay | Upwork, Fiverr | Maximum revenue capture; both sides feel the cost |
| Buyer membership | ClassPass, Amazon Prime | Strong retention; requires critical mass of supply |

### 3. Key Recommendations for the Swedish Market

1. **Start with low/no barriers for creators.** The Swedish creative services market is smaller than US; you need supply first. Consider free tier or very low subscription (149-199 SEK/month).

2. **Commission at 10-15% is competitive.** Below Fiverr (20%) and Airbnb Experiences (20%), in line with Upwork (~10%) and Eventbrite (~10-14%).

3. **"First-visit only" commission is compelling.** Fresha and Timma prove this works in Scandinavia. Charge 15-20% only when the platform delivers a genuinely new client.

4. **Consider buyer membership as Phase 2.** ClassPass proves it works for experiences/wellness. A "Usha Pass" at 99-199 SEK/month could give audience members credits, early access, and discounts — but only after you have sufficient creator supply.

5. **Bundle fees into displayed prices (DICE approach).** Swedish consumers expect transparent pricing. Avoid surprise checkout fees.

6. **Tiered creator plans unlock premium features, not lower commission.** Bokadirekt model: higher tiers = more visibility, marketing tools, priority support — not lower commission rates.
